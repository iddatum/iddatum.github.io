Hi, I’m Ingrid.

I’m a data analyst and business-systems professional with a background that sits somewhere between data, economics, reporting, and software. In other words, I like finding the story hidden inside messy information — and then turning it into something people can actually use.

My experience includes data quality, SQL reporting, dashboard development, KPI tracking, business requirements, user acceptance testing, and process documentation. I’ve worked with enterprise data, learning management systems, labour market reporting, master data management, and business intelligence tools. I’m comfortable moving between technical work and stakeholder conversations, which means I can write the query, validate the data, explain the result, and document the process without disappearing into a cave.

I recently completed a Bachelor of Science in General Computer Science at the University of Windsor, adding formal software and systems training to my earlier graduate background in Economics and Business. That combination has shaped the way I work: analytical, practical, detail-oriented, and always interested in making processes cleaner than I found them.

Over the years, I’ve used tools including SQL, Power BI, Tableau, SAS, Excel, Python, Java, C, Git, Jira, Teradata, GCP, Oracle databases, and reporting platforms such as SSRS and Amazon QuickSight. I especially enjoy work that involves cleaning up confusing data, building useful reports, improving workflows, and helping teams make better decisions with better information.

Outside of the technical details, I’m someone who likes solving problems, learning new systems, and asking the questions that make a project clearer. I care about accuracy, but I also care about making the final result understandable. Good data work should not require a decoder ring.

This site is a place for my portfolio, projects, resume, and notes as I continue building at the intersection of data analytics, software, and business systems.
